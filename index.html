Fact: Penguins are
awesome.

Why?

Glad you asked. Keep scrolling.

First: They’re deceptive little buggers.

Penguins are just cute, innocent birds that awkwardly waddle around, right?

WRONG.

Penguins are smooth, efficient killing machines. They know what they need to do, and they get it done.

Bullshit. Prove it.

Seriously! Killing machines.

Scientists put cameras on the backs of penguins, collected over 14 hours of footage from 11 penguins, and not once did they see a penguin miss its target. Penguins are not to be messed with (especially if you’re a tiny, tasty sea creature). Watch the video if you don’t believe me.

Second: They’re classy as hell.

Are you wearing a tuxedo 24-7?

I didn’t think so.

Not only are they ruthless killing machines, they’re well-dressed killing machines.

They’re the James Bond of birds.

Seriously?

The black back and white belly penguins are famous for is called countershading.

It’s a form of camouflage that lets penguins blend in from above (with the dark ocean bottom) and below (with the sunlit water).

Sneaky!

Third:They stand unflinching in the face of death, danger and dull days.

Kids are always a full-time job, but who wants to battle freezing temperatures, vicious seals, egg-stealing baby-penguin-eating birds, and did I mention the freezing temperatures?

Even after they’re born, young chicks left out in the open can freeze to death in less than two minutes. The parents must keep them tucked close and off the ground just like the egg.

Some penguins have to walk for days or weeks to hit the ocean while their mate remains at the nest, without food, battling weather and those troublesome egg-eating birds. That’s some hardcore dedication, and they’re damn good at it. CONSTANT VIGILANCE!

Oh just tell me more already.

Many penguins have to wait without food and water for their mate to arrive. The most extreme situation is that of the Emperor penguin. The males guard their egg for about 2 months while the female feeds. Adding in time for travel and courtship, it can be as many as 115 days before he can feed again. Emperor males have been known to drop as much as 44lbs (20kg), or about 45% of their body weight, in that time. These guys put up with a lot.

Fourth:They don’t like stereotypes.

The biggest and baddest penguins say fuck you and your gender stereotypes.

All penguins share at least some child-raising duties, and some reverse them entirely, forcing the male to stay home with the egg while the female feeds.

Say what?

Emperor Penguins, the largest of all penguins, completely reverse stereotypical parenting roles. The male must stay at home for months, keeping the egg warm and protected at all times, while the female feeds.

If the chick hatches before she returns, the male will feed the chick with a milk-like substance. (Birds don’t have mammary glands, but it's a secretion of protein and fat.)

Fifth:They’re sensible.

Antarctic penguins are equipped with exactly what they need to survive: Plenty of fat and feathers to keep them warm (or release heat when they’re too hot. Yes, it does happen sometimes.) Their circulatory system also adjusts to help keep their temperature regulated.

Tell me more.

  • Underneath is a layer of soft down feathers and under that a thick layer of fat. This keeps the penguins so warm they will actually fluff their feather to released trapped heat in order to cool down. -
  • Penguins circulatory systems can adjust conserving or releasing heat to keep the temperature constant.

Sixth:They’re not afraid to be different.

Some penguins get wild and crazy and spice up their formal black tie look with bright colors. (Except by bright colors I really just mean crazy yellow feathers sticking out of their head.)

Check out that sweet yellow headdress. And if you think that’s crazy looking, check out this guy.

MOAR SCIENCE PLZ.

I haven’t been able to find much science at all actually. Crested penguins belong to the genus Eudyptes. With five different crested species, it’s actually the most diverse group of penguins. The yellow feathers are thought to have evolved from a long-extinct ancestor.

Seventh: They’re resourceful.

Penguins are one of the few creatures living in Antarctica, a continent that’s 98% ice. It takes a lot to survive there.

Fascinating. I’d like some more words now.

  • Living near a continent that is 98% covered with ice, they must find bare land with small rocks to build a nest and raise their young.
  • The emperor penguin breeds in the coldest environment of any bird species; air temperatures may reach -40° (F/C), and wind speeds may reach 89 miles per hour (144 km/hr)! -

Eighth: They’re faster than a speeding bullet.

Okay fine, not quite that fast. But they can be pretty darn fast. They’re lulling you into a false sense of security with their adorable hopping and waddling. Rumor has it some penguins can even outrun a person over short distances.

You think I’m joking?

  • Penguins swim faster and dive deeper than all other birds. Some species can swim up to 22mph (36 kph). Adelie penguins can accelerate from 0 to 16 mph in less than one second when they leap into the water.
  • Penguins can jump about 6-7ft (2m) out of the water. Pretty impressive.
  • They all look clumsy on land, but Gentoo penguins supposedly can out-run a man over short distances. (I’m a little skeptical about this one, but until someone conclusively says otherwise, it stays. :P )

Ninth: Teamwork is second nature for them.

Penguins know how to work together and get shit done.

Some penguins, instead of finding their own individual paths to the sea, take the same path and wear down a nice, simple trail for everyone. None of that “every man for himself” crap. Others huddle together in groups to preserve body heat.

I’d like some proper information please.

  • Gentoo and Macaroni penguins are among those who establish commonly used paths, making it easier for everyone to use.
  • Emperor penguins huddle in groups (often in the thousands) in order to conserve body heat. They also make sure they move so those standing on the outside cycle inside to warm up, preventing any one penguin from becoming too cold.

Tenth: When they make a commitment, they mean it.

Unlike most birds, many species of penguins tend to be monogamous. This means year after year, even after swimming hundreds of thousands of miles away, many partners find each other again among thousands of virtually identical birds.

I demand science.

  • Scientists have tracked a pair of Magellanic penguins who have been together for 16 years. That’s better than a lot of marriages these days!
  • 80%–90% of African Penguins return to the same partner year after year unless forced to change due to death or a partner's disappearance.

So let’s summarize:

They’re the James Bond of birds, just without the obnoxious sexist bits. They work together, like to throw a little color in with all that black and white, pretty fast when they want to be, and they’re determined to survive in the harshest climate on earth. Anything but average.

In other words, awesome.
